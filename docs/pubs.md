<h2>Leveraging clinical sleep data across multiple pediatric cohorts for insights into neurodevelopment: the Retrospective Analysis of Sleep in Pediatric (RASP) cohorts study </h2>
<div class="img-text">
  <div class="img-col">
    <img src="../img/rasp-stager.png" alt="">
  </div>
  <div class="text-col">
    <p><strong> Gong et al (SLEEP, 2026) </strong> [<a href="https://academic.oup.com/sleep/article-abstract/49/2/zsaf157/8158645?login=false">PDF</a>]</p>
    <p>

<h2>Electroencephalographic Microstates During Sleep and Wake in Schizophrenia</h2>

<div class="img-text">

  <div class="img-col">
    <img src="../img/microstate2.png" alt="EEG microstates figure">
  </div>

  <div class="text-col">
    <p><strong>Murphy et al. (Biological Psychiatry GOS, 2024)</strong> [<a href="https://zzz.bwh.harvard.edu/pdfs/murphy-microstates-2024.pdf">PDF</a>]</p>

    <p>
      We analyzed high-density EEG sleep data from 114 patients with schizophrenia
      and 79 control participants to characterize microstates during sleep and wake.
      Results reveal behavioral state–dependent cortical dysconnectivity in schizophrenia,
      largely independent of traditional sleep EEG markers such as reduced spindles.
    </p>
  </div>

<h2>Mapping neurodevelopment with sleep macro- and micro-architecture across multiple pediatric populations</h2>

<div class="img-text">
  <div class="img-col">
    <img src="../img/sleep-devel.png" alt="Sleep development figure">
  </div>
  <div class="text-col">
    <p><strong>Kozhemiako et al. (NeuroImage: Clinical, 2023)</strong> [<a href="https://zzz.bwh.harvard.edu/pdfs/kozhemiako-2024-ped.pdf">PDF</a>]</p>
    <p>  To build and evaluate a sleep-based, quantitative metric of brain maturation, we used PSG data from large research
      and clinical samples spanning childhood and adolescence (N = 4,013, aged 2.5 to 17.5 years). Our results indicate
      that sleep architecture offers a sensitive window for characterizing brain maturation, suggesting the potential
      for scalable, objective sleep-based biomarkers to measure neurodevelopment.
    </p>
  </div>
</div>


<h2>A Potential Source of Bias in Group-Level EEG Microstate Analysis</h2>

<div class="img-text">
  <div class="img-col">
    <img src="../img/microstate1.png" alt="EEG microstate bias figure">
  </div>
  <div class="text-col">
    <p><strong>Murphy et al. (Brain Topography, 2023)</strong> [<a href="https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10548-023-00992-7">PDF</a>]</p>
    <p>
      Microstate analysis is a promising technique for analyzing high-density electroencephalographic data, but there are
      multiple questions about methodological best practices. Our results suggest that even subtle chance differences in
      microstate topography can have profound effects on derived microstate metrics and that future studies using
      microstate analysis should take steps to mitigate this large source of error.
    </p>
  </div>
</div>

<h2>Common polygenic variation contributes to risk of schizophrenia and bipolar disorder</h2>

<div class="img-text">
  <div class="img-col">
    <img src="../img/polygenic.png" alt="Polygenic risk figure">
  </div>
  <div class="text-col">
    <p><strong>International Schizophrenia Consortium (Nature, 2009)</strong> [ <a href="https://zzz.nyspi.org/library/isc-2009.pdf">PDF</a>]</p>
    <p>
      One of the first large-scale genome-wide association studies of schizophrenia, here we made the observation that
      many common variants of very small effect appear to contribute to the heritability of schizophrenia. Most of these
      risk alleles are individually unlikely to be detectable, at least at currently feasible sample sizes.
    </p>
